Living in Cookeville

Cookeville-Putnam County is a natural fit.

From majestic waterfalls and state parks to a historic downtown square, art and cultural events, and an easy pace of life — this is a place to put down roots and make memories. Here's a taste of what's around the corner.

Downtown
The Historic Square
Locally owned boutiques, coffee at Poet's, scoops at Cream City Ice Cream, and hidden murals around every corner of the West Side district.
Outdoors
Waterfalls & State Parks
Cummins Falls, Burgess Falls, and Window Cliffs are minutes away — plus trails, kayaking, and camping across the Upper Cumberland's parks and natural areas.
Education
Tennessee Tech
A respected public university anchors the city — bringing jobs, sports, and cultural events, and keeping Cookeville young and growing.
Community
Festivals & Dogwood Park
A beautifully kept downtown park hosts free summer movies and concerts, the fall ART Prowl, the Putnam County Chuckwagon Festival, and the holiday light show.
Connected
Halfway on I-40
About 80 miles to Nashville and 100 to Knoxville — close enough for a day trip or a commute, far enough to skip the congestion.
Everyday
Local Flavor
Ralph's Donut Shop, Red Silo Brewing, Tennessee Legend Distillery, the Upper Cumberland Farmers Market — small-town favorites worth the trip.
